Project Name: Chem-E-Car 2017 Competition
Date Initiated: September, 2016
Date Completed: April, 2017
What: I competed in the 2017 AIChe Chem-E-Car Competition in Bellingham, WA as the Reactor Team Lead. Our team of 18 members started developing the car in September, 2016. The goal of our project was to create a shoebox-sized car that would travel a set distance, carrying a set weight of water. The more accurate our car's travelled distance was to the given value, the higher we placed. We were not allowed to use any devices to measure the distance travelled, nor use a commercial battery to power the motor, meaning that we had to build our own chemical stopping mechanism and chemical power source for the car. More details about the competition can be found here. As Reactor Team Lead, I was in charge of developing the reactor that held the chemical timer for the car. I had to implement placement for the sensors and circuitry, create and maintain optimal conditions for the chemical reaction and ensure stability of the reactor onto the car when the car is mobile.

Project Name: Chem-E-Car Fuel Cell
Date Initiated: June, 2017
Date Completed: August, 2017
What: I worked on connecting a Hydrogen fuel cell to the car within a team of three. After doing research about how to implement a fuel cell, I did research about Hydrogen-producing Electrolyzers. With the features of the Electrolyzer having to match the specifications of the motor, the tubing and the Fuel Cell, I took into account variables like voltage output from the Fuel Cell, required voltage input to the Electrolyzer, and tubing diameter. After I ordered an Electrolyzer online from the The Fuel Cell Store, our team started to design apparatus to transport Hydrogen gas from an Electrolyzer into a Fuel Cell on a moving car. Because the Electrolyzer is too big, we cannot put it on the car. We would need to generate Hydrogen, put it into temporary storage, then attach that storage to the car to feed Hydrogen to the fuel cell. We ended up transporting the gas with a balloon and succeeded in moving the car for two minutes, which is a lot more time than we need.

Company Name: BCIT Chemical Sciences
Position Title: Co-op Student, Research Assistant
Date Initiated: August, 2017
Date Completed: In Progress
What: I am currently a research assistant at BCIT Chemical Sciences, working on research and development for the pulp and paper industry. Working primarily with thermo- mechanical pulp, my goal is to find the optimum conditions at which Chlorine Dioxide will increase pulp brightness and decrease loss in tensile strength when peroxide and caustic is added. I have learned how to make paper hand sheets out of pulp by controlling the consistency of the pulp mixture. I have also learned how to use machines that test hand sheet properties, including Tensile testers, Tear strength testers, Brightness testers and Thickness testers. Because I am in research and development, one of my main duties is to chemically treat pulp with varying temperature, consistency and time conditions against a non-treated control. Because I have to collect a variety of consistently updated data, I have to format my own spreadsheet to accommodate it. I am helping my supervisor publish a paper in order to provide pulp and paper companies with accurate data on Chlorine Dioxide, their frequently used bleaching agent.
